Statue of Ramses II

This 3,200-year-old statue of Ramses II was relocated to the GEM in 2018 and now stands prominently in the museum's atrium

Statue of Queen Hatshepsut

A statue of one of Egypt's few female pharaohs, Hatshepsut, is part of the GEM's collection

Tutankhamun’s Golden Mask

This iconic funerary mask of the boy king Tutankhamun is part of the complete Tutankhamun collection being transferred to the GEM .

Hang Obelisk

The Hanging Obelisk is one of the key architectural features at the GEM and is not an ancient artifact, but a modern display element with deep symbolic meaning.

Golden Sarcophagus of Tutankhamun

The Golden Sarcophagus of Tutankhamun is a lavish, solid gold coffin that housed the young pharaoh's mummy, symbolizing his divine status and the wealth of 18th Dynasty Egypt.

Tutankhamun’s Royal Jewelry

A collection of ornate jewelry pieces, including necklaces, bracelets, and rings, showcasing the craftsmanship and opulence of the New Kingdom

4,000-Year-Old Preserved Crocodile

A remarkably preserved crocodile mummy, exemplifying the ancient Egyptians' reverence for the Nile and its creatures.

Khufu's solar boat

The Khufu Boat is a 4,600-year-old, fully preserved cedarwood funerary ship built for Pharaoh Khufu, symbolizing his journey with the sun god Ra in the afterlife

Tutankhamun’s Chariots

These chariots, discovered in Tutankhamun's tomb, are among the artifacts being moved to the GEM
