About us

In January 2002, Egypt launched a global competition to design a new museum complex near the Pyramids of Giza, aimed at preserving and showcasing the country's vast ancient treasures. A foundation stone was laid a month later, and in 2003, Irish firm Heneghan Peng Architects won the design contract for what would become the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM).


Construction began in 2005 but faced years of delays due to environmental, financial, and political issues—particularly during the Arab Spring in 2011, which halted progress and hurt tourism revenue.


After stability returned in 2014, work resumed with the support of international funding. Now expected to open in mid-2024, the GEM will be the world’s largest archaeological museum, housing over 100,000 artifacts. For the first time, King Tutankhamun’s entire collection will be displayed, alongside pieces spanning prehistoric times to the Greek and Roman periods.


Earlier opening dates in 2018 and 2019 were postponed, but the current timeline appears realistic.
